# Materials guideline for burns

The main burn material should be predictable and clean-burning to as high a degree as possible.

## Preferred materials

- Dry structural wood.
- Dry framing lumber.
- Known untreated wood.
- Clean natural materials in limited amounts.

## Materials to avoid

- Rotten wood.
- Wet wood.
- Cardboard as a primary material.
- Pressure-treated wood.
- Painted wood.
- Plastic.
- Foam.
- Rubber.
- PVC.
- Synthetic fabrics.
- Batteries.
- Electronics.
- Sealed containers.
- Unknown salvaged materials.
- Materials likely to throw embers unpredictably.

## Important considerations

- Flying sparks are affected by material choice. Rotten wood and crumbling material can increase ember risk.
- Dry wood still throws burning particles, especially in wind or structures with height.
- Forest risk must not be dismissed just because the audience perimeter is adequate.
